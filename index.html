Musical Notes

The musical alphabet has 7 letters that repeat in a pattern.

  • A, B, C, D, E, F, G
  • After G, the pattern starts again from A at a higher pitch
  • These notes form the foundation of all music

Major Scales

A scale is a series of notes arranged in ascending or descending order.

C Major Scale Example:

C - D - E - F - G - A - B - C

This scale has no sharps or flats, making it the easiest to learn.

Understanding Chords

Chords are formed when multiple notes are played together.

  • Major Chords: Sound bright and happy (Example: C-E-G)
  • Minor Chords: Sound darker and sad (Example: A-C-E)
